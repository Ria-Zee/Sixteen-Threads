## The Kingdom Factions

### Oyo Successor States — "The Iron Memory"
**Leader:** Alaafin Adewale III, a king who inherited a fragmented empire and spends most of his energy preventing the fragments from becoming permanent  
**Current posture:** Defensive mobilization. The Oyo cavalry units — the most feared in the region — have been recalled from their posting routes and positioned along the eastern roads toward Nri territory.  
**Public reason:** "We protect our remaining borders."  
**Actual reason:** Akin Adesanya is not the only person from Oyo who has lost someone to a "peace failure." The Alaafin remembers the last breakdown before the Covenant held. He is not going to be surprised again.  
**What they want:** Restoration of the Aso-Ijo, acknowledgment that whoever broke it bears culpability, and a formal renewal ceremony with all seven kingdoms present. They will accept a negotiated peace. They will not accept ambiguity about who did this.

### Asante Confederacy — "The Golden Anchor"
**Leader:** Asantehene Kwame Boateng, paramount chief, presiding over a confederation that has survived four centuries by being the most diplomatically sophisticated kingdom on the continent  
**Current posture:** Recall of all Asafo military companies from trade routes, framed publicly as "scheduled rotation"  
**Public reason:** Scheduled rotation.  
**Actual reason:** The Fontomfrom war drums were beaten for three hours last Tuesday. They have not been beaten in sixty years. Somebody made that decision and it was not scheduled.  
**What they want:** An investigation with results before any mobilization becomes irreversible. The Asante are the kingdom most likely to wait — they have the most to lose from a war that disrupts trade, and the most institutional memory of what wars cost.  
**Crucial detail:** Kofi Mensah-Asante carries unofficial authority from the Asantehene — not to negotiate, but to observe and report. His priestly role is real. His secondary role as the Asantehene's eyes is also real. He has not mentioned the second role.

### Nri Kingdom — "The Iron Claim"
**Leader:** Eze Obi Nwosu, a king of unusual political intelligence and unusual willingness to act on incomplete information  
**Current posture:** Warrior-priests (Nze) mobilized along the northern border, facing east in the direction of Oyo territory  
**Public reason:** "Precaution against covenant-break chaos."  
**Actual reason:** Eze Nwosu received a communication from Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun six months ago outlining their plan and inviting Nri's participation. His response was non-committal — he has been gathering intelligence since. He does not yet know whether Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun succeeded or whether the results match what they promised.  
**What they want:** If Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun's plan works as described — a merger of Aye and Orun that ends death — Nri gains spiritual supremacy over all seven kingdoms, since Nri's traditional authority derives from their connection to the spirit realm. If it doesn't work, they need to not be the kingdom that was associated with it.  
**The nail:** The Nri nail in the Fire Keep is forty years old — it predates Eze Nwosu's reign. He did not put it there. Someone from Nri did, forty years ago. This is a separate mystery nested inside the main one.

### Zulu Kingdom — "The Road-Mappers"
**Leader:** King Cetshwayo Dlamini II (fictional; no relation to Thandiwe despite the surname — it is common)  
**Current posture:** Scouts mapping roads to Ori-Oke. Not military roads — the scouts are cartographers, not soldiers.  
**What they want:** The Zulu scouts who visited Ori-Oke before the party have not yet reported. They were supposed to report ten days ago. The road-mapping is not preparation for war. It is preparation for a retrieval mission. Something happened to the first scouts at Ori-Oke and the king is quietly preparing to get them back without anyone knowing he sent them in the first place.

### Mandinka Confederacy — "The Speaking Silence"
**Leader:** Mansa Diabaté, a ruler who communicates almost entirely through his griot court, which is either profound or cowardly depending on who you ask  
**Current posture:** Diplomatic envoys dispatched to every kingdom, including Ile-Olu. Seku is the most senior of these envoys.  
**What they want:** Officially — information. The Mandinka griot tradition's deepest fear is a world-event they failed to record. They cannot let this covenant break pass undocumented. Unofficially — if the realms merge and the tradition of oral memory becomes irrelevant (because everything is directly accessible), the griots lose their entire cultural function. Seku is carrying this fear, privately, and it shapes every decision he makes.

## [SEALED] Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun — "New Light"

**Type:** Trans-continental ideological faction  
**Founded:** Approximately 60 years ago  
**Size:** Unknown — estimated 40-80 active members across multiple kingdoms, plus an unknown number of sympathizers in positions of institutional authority  
**Base:** No fixed location; they operate through a network of "still houses" — unremarkable buildings in market towns that serve as meeting points and message relays  
**Symbol:** A thread being pulled from a piece of cloth — always drawn as a single clean line, never completed

**Founding ideology:** The Aso-Ijo was woven as a separation. They believe separation was the original mistake. Their founding document (a text called *Iwe Tuntun* — The New Book, written by their founder whose name is the one excised from Ori-Oke's records) argues that mortality, grief, and the limitation of human knowledge all derive from the severing of Aye from Orun. Merge the realms, and humans gain access to all ancestral knowledge, the dead return, and the suffering caused by endings ends. The text is philosophically sophisticated and genuinely compassionate. Its conclusions are catastrophic.

**Why their plan fails (GM knowledge):** The Aso-Ijo doesn't just separate the realms — it *filters* them. Orun is not a peaceful place. It is an accurate one, where everything is exactly what it is without mercy or pretense. The gods in Orun are not kind, patient beings — they are enormous, old, and structured around a cosmology that does not include human comfort as a design principle. A merger does not produce a world where the dead return and everyone has access to ancestral wisdom. It produces a world where the full unfiltered content of Orun overlaps with Aye — where the gods walk in markets, where the weight of every accumulated human memory presses on every living moment, where the dead do return but as exactly what they were, including their suffering. The world becomes, simultaneously, everything that has ever happened. It is not survivable.

**Current operation:** The cloth has been damaged. The missing section is in Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun's possession. Their plan requires the tear to be enlarged to a specific threshold before the natural reweaving process (which begins slowly after any damage) seals it. They are working against the clock. If the party restores Mama Moremi and she can initiate emergency repairs, the plan fails. This is why her spirit was taken — not as a punishment, but as a delay mechanism. She is being held in Orun, not harmed, but unable to return without help.

**Leadership:** Unknown to all player-accessible sources. Kofi suspects a god is involved. He is not entirely wrong — one minor deity, Ogun-Ibi (a shadow-aspect of the iron god Ogun not commonly worshipped) has provided Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun with the ability to straddle the boundary. But this deity is not a leader. It is a tool they found and learned to use.

**Relationship to Seku:** Seku is not a member. He is a conduit between Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun and Eze Nwosu, a role he was assigned without being asked. He has been trying to determine whether Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun is dangerous enough to stop or functional enough to negotiate with. He has recently, privately, concluded they are dangerous enough to stop. He has not yet decided this is more important than his loyalty to Eze Nwosu.
