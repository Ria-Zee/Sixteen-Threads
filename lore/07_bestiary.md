## Boundary-Straddlers (Ìrìnnà Àárọ̀ — "Dawn Crossers")

**Observable behavior:** Humanoid in form but wrong in subtle ways. They cast no shadow. Their movements are slightly delayed from where they appear to be — as if their body and their position in space have a 0.3-second disagreement. They do not speak in the conventional sense — when they communicate, the words arrive in your mind approximately one second before their mouth moves. In daylight they are nearly invisible, present only as a heat-distortion shimmer. At night they are visible as slightly luminous outlines, like a body remembered by the air it displaced.

**Nature:** Humans who are straddling the boundary between Aye and Orun — not dead, not fully alive. They volunteered for this state as Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun operatives. The process involves swallowing a preparation made from the Aso-Ijo's discarded thread fragments (from historical repairs) combined with a specific botanical compound. The result is a person who exists in both realms simultaneously, able to pass through the tear without enlarging it, able to carry physical objects between realms.

**[SEALED] Full nature:** The straddling state is not permanent and not stable. After approximately six months in this condition, the person's identity begins to fragment — they retain memories but lose the emotional connections between memories. After twelve months, they retain information but cannot form new experiences. The Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun leadership knows this. The operatives were not told. Many of the current straddlers have been in this state for over a year. They are completing their assigned missions with the emotional investment of people who no longer fully feel the stakes. This makes them more dangerous, not less — they have no hesitation.

**Weaknesses:** Iron disrupts their dual-state temporarily — direct contact with forged iron forces them fully into Aye for approximately thirty seconds. Akin's glaive is extremely effective. [SEALED] The disruption also causes them pain — the only pain they can still feel. An ethical player (guided by Thandiwe and Kofi) can use this to incapacitate rather than kill, which matters because these are human beings in a tragic condition, not supernatural enemies.

**Encounter behavior:**
- Do not initiate combat unless their message delivery is interrupted
- Will sacrifice themselves to ensure a message arrives
- Can be reasoned with if addressed in the language of their original kingdom (they retain language longer than most other capacities)
- Seku can communicate with them effectively — he has been doing so, and feels increasing guilt about the state they are in

## The Holding Court Guardians (Àwọn Olùṣọ — "The Watchers")

**Observable behavior:** Encountered only in Orun, in the Holding Court where Moremi's spirit is kept. They appear as very tall figures — approximately 2.5 meters — wearing garments that shift between the funeral dress of all seven kingdoms simultaneously, cycling through styles as if unable to choose. Their faces are present but feature-indistinct, like a face remembered from a dream. They do not speak. They communicate by arrangement — they position themselves in patterns that, if read correctly, are sentences.

**Nature:** Not malevolent. They are functionaries — spirits assigned to the Holding Court as custodians. Their role is to ensure displaced spirits do not wander from the court into the deeper reaches of Orun. They did not displace Moremi — they found her there when she arrived and have been maintaining her as they would maintain any court resident. They are following procedure. The procedure does not include releasing her without authorization.

**[SEALED] How to navigate them:**
- They read intent, not action. A visitor who enters with purely compassionate intent will be allowed to move through the court without interference.
- They read deception immediately — anyone carrying a lie or a hidden agenda will find the court rearranging itself, corridors extending, the destination retreating.
- Thandiwe, whose intent is clean (she is there to heal, period), can move through freely. Seku, if he accompanies her, would struggle — he is still carrying concealed information.
- Kofi cannot enter the Holding Court at all — the guardians will not admit a living diviner, because a living diviner in Orun creates a channel the ancestors can use to communicate unsupervised, and the guardians' function includes preventing unsupervised ancestor-communication in the court's jurisdiction.
- Authorization to release Moremi: any person holding her covenant object (the carved figure) can release her thread. The guardians will honor this — it overrides their procedural mandate. The carved figure is in Nri. This is the core logistical problem of Act 3.

## Ogun-Ibi (The Shadow-Aspect of Iron)

**Observable behavior:** Not encountered directly in normal gameplay. Its presence is felt: iron behaves wrong near it. Akin's blade vibrates. Nails in walls lean toward it. The seventh mirror in the House of Voices, which shows Orun, shows its shadow as a large darkness in the background of Moremi's holding room. It is not moving. It is waiting for something to move.

**Nature:** A minor deity — not worshipped in any mainstream tradition, known only to specialists. Ogun, the Yoruba god of iron and war, has many aspects: Ogun the Smith, Ogun the Warrior, Ogun the Road-Opener. Ogun-Ibi is the shadow-aspect — what iron becomes when it is used purely for control rather than craft or defense. It is not evil in a cosmic sense. It is iron's capacity for domination, made sentient. In ordinary times it has no power — iron used for war or craft keeps it dormant. The Aso-Ijo's damage has given it room to operate by weakening the filters between realms.

**[SEALED] Its role:** Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun did not summon Ogun-Ibi. They discovered it — a minor deity with grievances and dormant power — and offered it something: if the realms merge, Ogun-Ibi's shadow-aspect becomes indistinguishable from the primary aspect. It ceases to be the shadow. It becomes simply Ogun. This is what it wants. It is not ideologically aligned with Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun — it does not care about mortality or human suffering. It wants to not be a shadow anymore. It provided the boundary-straddling technique. It holds Moremi's thread indirectly — Elder Okafor is the proximate holder, but Ogun-Ibi is the power source making the binding work at distance.

**Confrontation:** Not a combat encounter. Ogun-Ibi cannot be killed — it is an aspect of a god. It can be reasoned with, or it can be made irrelevant. Kofi's argument: if the realms merge, Ogun-Ibi gets what it wants but also gets absorbed into everything else. There is no "primary Ogun" in a merged world, only undifferentiated divine noise. It has been promised something that does not actually exist as described. This argument, properly delivered by a senior Okomfo with appropriate ritual framing, will cause Ogun-Ibi to withdraw its support from Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun. Without the boundary-straddling technique, their current operatives are stranded. The operation stalls. This is Kofi's moment — the thing the entire journey has been building toward.

## The Confused Dead (Àwọn Tó Padà — "Those Who Returned")

**Observable behavior:** The people seen in villages along the road who seem slightly wrong. The grandmother who has been dead for six years sitting in her garden, confused about what year it is. The warrior who fought in the last border skirmish who has walked back from Orun and is asking where his commanding officer is. They are not threatening. They are deeply, specifically disorienting — they remember everything about their lives up to the moment of death with perfect clarity, and cannot understand why the world has changed.

**Nature:** The Aso-Ijo's damage has created a slow leak — spirits who were near the boundary in Orun, or who had strong emotional connections to Aye, have been gradually slipping back through the tear. Not through any intention on their part. They fell through.

**[SEALED] Their significance:** The confused dead are the most visible symptom and also the most important witness pool. They were in Orun. They passed through the tear recently. They saw Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun's operatives working on the boundary from the Orun side. The specific returned spirit in the Day Two village — an elder named Baba Kunle, who died eight years ago — was standing in the Holding Court when Moremi's spirit arrived. He saw who delivered her and heard what instructions were given. He has been trying to tell people. Nobody has asked him the right questions. Zawadi, if she thinks to apply her danger-pattern methodology to a witness interview, can extract a full description of Moremi's captor. The captor's description matches Elder Chibuike Okafor of Nri.

## The Unseen Cartographers (Àwọn Àwòrán — "The Map-Makers")

**Observable behavior:** Not encountered directly. Evidence of their presence: the Day Four forest where trees are in the wrong arrangement. River crossings with temperature anomalies. Roads that curve toward destinations the traveler did not choose. The world's geography has been subtly, systematically remapped.

**[SEALED] Nature:** Not creatures — a process. The boundary damage is causing Orun's geography to assert itself over Aye's. Orun is accurate — it shows things as they are, not as they were arranged. The "remapped" elements are places where Orun's version of the landscape is more persistent than Aye's. The trees are in the wrong arrangement from a human perspective; they are in the correct arrangement from the perspective of the land's oldest memory. The roads curve toward Ori-Oke because Ori-Oke is where the damage is, and Orun's geography organizes itself around points of significance. The world is not being attacked. It is reorienting toward its wound. This is, if anything, helpful — Adaeze's party arrived on the Northern Path "chosen" for them because Orun's geography organized itself to bring help toward the shrine as efficiently as possible. The world wants to be healed. It is doing what it can.
