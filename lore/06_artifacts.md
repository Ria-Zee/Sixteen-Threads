## The Aso-Ijo (The Covenant Cloth) — Primary Artifact

**Physical description:** Nine meters of silk-cotton weave, threads so fine they are difficult to count with the naked eye. Color: white at the center deepening to deep indigo at both edges — not dyed, the color is structural, encoded in the thread material itself. Smells faintly of rain on dry earth regardless of ambient weather. Weight: surprisingly light for its size, approximately 400 grams. When held near a flame the threads do not burn — they vibrate slightly, as if the heat is a kind of music they recognize.

**Known properties:**
- Physical barrier between Aye and Orun when intact
- Each thread encodes a specific covenant promise — touching a thread while in a meditative state allows a diviner to read the promise it holds
- The cloth cannot be cut by any mortal blade — the tear was made by something that operates between realms
- The cloth regenerates slowly after damage — approximately 1 centimeter of new thread per week, grown from the intact edges inward

**Current damage:** 40cm diagonal tear (upper left quadrant) + one palm-sized section missing (lower center). The missing section's removal was surgical — the surrounding threads were unraveled six centimeters back on each side, then the section lifted free.

**[SEALED] The replacement thread:** A foreign fiber, inserted into the unraveled area after the section was removed. Indistinguishable by sight — identical color, similar weight. Identifiable only by: (1) Thandiwe's herbal knowledge (the fiber was treated with a preservative compound not used in traditional West African textile work), (2) Kofi's spiritual reading (the thread has no memory — all authentic Aso-Ijo threads carry emotional residue; this one is blank, which is impossible for something this old), (3) Zawadi's pattern analysis (the insertion technique matches a textile forgery method documented in one Kikuyu record from 200 years ago, used to falsify land-covenant documents).

**[SEALED] What the missing section contains:** The geographic covenant record — each of the seven kingdoms' territorial agreements, compressed into thread. Without it, the Aso-Ijo can be patched but the territorial covenants cannot be restored. This means even after repair, the kingdoms' borders become legally ambiguous in spiritual law — which is exactly the kind of instability that justifies indefinite renegotiation. Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun does not only want the realms merged. They want the political framework of the covenant dismantled first.

## The Nri Nail — Evidence Artifact

**Physical description:** Iron nail, 18cm long, octagonal cross-section (Nri forge signature), carbon-rich alloy (slightly darker than Oyo or Mandinka ironwork). Upper third has a specific spiral twist pattern — three rotations, left-handed — that is the mark of the Nri forge school active between 60 and 80 years ago. Base shows oxidation consistent with 40 years of exposure to a humid environment, then 3-4 weeks in dry ash.

**Known properties:**
- In Igbo spiritual tradition: a claim marker. Driving iron into a sacred fire means "this sacred space is now under our jurisdiction."
- It is not a weapon. It is a legal document made of iron.
- It is aggressive. It is not violent. The distinction matters in Igbo law and is irrelevant in Oyo military tradition.

**What it tells us (assembled from all agents' observations):**
- Made 40-60 years ago (Thandiwe dates it; Zawadi confirms against forging records)
- Made by a Nri smith of the now-defunct Ezumezu forge school (Akin knows this school; he says nothing about why)
- Placed within the last 3-4 weeks (Thandiwe's environmental analysis)
- Placed carefully, deliberately, by someone who knew the symbolic meaning (the vertical precision, the center placement)

**[SEALED] The Ezumezu forge school:** Closed 35 years ago when its master smith was accused of producing materials for an unnamed political faction and fled rather than face the Eze's judgment. The unnamed faction, in the internal Nri record, is referred to only as "the New Thinkers." This is Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun's earliest recorded name. The nail was not placed by Eze Nwosu. It was placed by Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun to implicate Nri — or more precisely, to make Nri's implication ambiguous enough that investigation would take months while the operation completed. It is a misdirection artifact.

## Moremi's Carved Figure — The Tether Object

**Physical description:** A small carved wooden figure, approximately 12cm tall, Yoruba style — simplified human form, no facial features, arms at sides, a small hollow in the chest cavity filled with a piece of red thread. The wood is very old — Moremi carved it herself thirty years ago from the shrine's iroko repair wood. It fits in a closed fist.

**Known properties:**
- A covenant object. In Yoruba spiritual tradition, when a person carves their own figure and gives it to another, they are creating a tether — a physical representation of their trust in that person, which also carries a fragment of their spiritual signature.
- The figure can be used to communicate across short spirit-distances — holder holds it, thinks a message, the maker feels it as a sensation.
- [SEALED] It can also be used to hold a person's spirit thread — if the holder recites a specific binding prayer while physically holding the figure, the maker's spirit cannot return to their body until the holder releases it. This is an obscure, rarely documented ability. Moremi did not know her figure could be used this way when she gave it away.

**[SEALED] Current holder:** Elder Chibuike Okafor of Nri, age 78. Moremi gave him the figure thirty years ago when he was a young priest visiting Ori-Oke on official Nri business. They developed a friendship — genuine, warm, spanning decades of occasional correspondence. He did not bind her spirit out of malice. He bound it because Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun told him it was necessary for the operation and that she would be released unharmed afterward. He believed them. He is currently in the Nri palace, holding the figure, increasingly aware that "unharmed afterward" was a promise from people who have given him no way to contact them for three weeks. He is frightened and does not know how to ask for help without confessing what he did.

## Seku's Kora — The Memory Instrument

**Physical description:** A 21-string kora — bridge-harp of the Mandinka tradition — gourd resonator wrapped in cowhide, hardwood neck, the strings made from fishing line of a specific weight. Seku's kora has been repaired eleven times and shows it: the gourd has three patches, the cowhide has been re-tensioned, two of the tuning handles are replacements from different wood. It is approximately 25 years old and sounds better than a new one because it has learned the player.

**Known properties:**
- When Seku plays, conversations stop. Not metaphorically — people physically stop speaking and turn toward the sound. This is not magic. It is a griot's trained instrument in the hands of a senior practitioner.
- The kora is Seku's memory system. He does not write. He encodes information in melodic phrases — specific sequences that correspond to facts, names, events. If you know how to listen, his playing is a recitation. Nobody in the current party knows how to listen. Yet.
- [SEALED] The kora has one encoded sequence Seku has never played for anyone: the full account of his king's communication with Ìmọlẹ̀ Tuntun, encoded melodically as insurance. If Seku dies or disappears, whoever holds the kora and knows the griot listening technique can decode it. This is Seku's contingency plan — his dead-man's switch. He has not told anyone the kora holds this. Zawadi has noticed he handles it differently from his other belongings.

## The Akrafokonmu Disc — Kofi's Divination Tool

**Physical description:** A circular brass disc, 8cm diameter, 4mm thick. Perforated in 23 specific locations — the perforation pattern is unique to the Mensah-Asante lineage, passed from Okomfo to Okomfo. When tilted toward a light source, the perforations cast specific shadow patterns. The disc is warm to the touch regardless of ambient temperature. It smells faintly of palm wine — an offering it absorbed during Kofi's initiation ceremony thirty-nine years ago.

**Known properties:**
- Primary divination tool. Kofi reads the shadow patterns as text — each configuration corresponds to an ancestral message or probability assessment.
- The disc shows what the ancestors choose to communicate. It cannot be forced to show something the ancestors are withholding.
- [SEALED] The disc has been showing the same shadow pattern for the last four days — a configuration Kofi has never seen before in thirty-nine years of practice. It does not correspond to any entry in his memorized pattern library. He has not mentioned this to anyone. He is spending the hours between midnight and dawn cross-referencing against everything he knows. He has concluded it is not a new pattern — it is an old pattern that has been *inverted*. An inverted pattern in Akan divination tradition means the message is coming from the opposite direction than expected. The ancestors are not sending this message. Someone in Orun is sending it to him from inside Orun, which means it is coming from someone who is currently there — either dead or displaced. Mama Moremi, held in the Holding Court, is trying to send the party a message through Kofi's own instrument.

## Ọdẹ-Owó (Hunter of Debts) — Akin's Glaive

**Physical description:** An iron-tipped glaive, haft 180cm of hardwood from the Oyo plateau, blade 35cm of smelted iron with a slight forward curve — designed for mounted cavalry use but balanced for dismounted combat by a modification Akin made himself. The blade is polished to a mirror finish. The haft is wrapped in indigo-dyed leather, now worn smooth at the grip points. The iron tip carries no inscriptions — Akin removed the original battle-prayers his father had engraved, not from disrespect, but because he did not want to inherit his father's prayers. He would make his own.

**Known properties:**
- A functional weapon. Also a ritual object — in Oyo tradition, a warrior's named weapon is a covenant between the warrior and Ogun, god of iron. The name "Hunter of Debts" is a statement of intent: Akin believes every unpaid obligation eventually demands payment.
- The blade reflects truly — no distortion. In the House of Voices, when Akin holds the blade near the seventh mirror (the one showing Orun), the blade's reflection shows the mirror's reflection of Orun, which is slightly different from what the mirror shows directly. One additional figure is visible in the blade's reflection that is not visible in the mirror. [SEALED: The figure is Mama Moremi, standing, watching. She can see through polished iron. This is Yoruba cosmological fact — Ogun controls iron, and through iron the spirit world and mortal world have always had a thin connection. Moremi knows the party is there. She has been watching since they arrived.]

## The Messenger's Staff — Adaeze's Diplomatic Immunity

**Physical description:** Two ivory rods, each 60cm, connected by a 15cm linking section of copper wire wound in a specific braid pattern — seven strands for seven kingdoms, each strand a different gauge. The braid is tight and precise. A craftsperson made this. The ivory is old — pre-covenant-era old, which means it predates the political structures that now honor it.

**Known properties:**
- Universal symbol of diplomatic immunity across all seven kingdoms. Recognized by every trained soldier, priest, and official across the covenant territory. Means: do not kill this person, they carry words.
- [SEALED] The staff is also a recording instrument — the copper wire braid carries a specific resonance. Words spoken in the holder's presence while the staff is held vertically are encoded in the wire's tension. A trained metalworker who knows the technique can read the tension as audio — a crude but functional recording. This function is not documented in any official capacity. Three High Council members know about it. They did not tell Adaeze. Every significant conversation the party has had since departure has been recorded in the wire. If the staff is recovered, the High Council will have a complete record of everything including Seku's confession and Kofi's private concerns. Whether this is surveillance or protection is a matter of interpretation.
